New rehabilitation tech helping veterans

28JAN

For veterans recovering from severe physical injuries, the road to rehabilitation can be long and arduous. However, a new wave of assistive technology is changing the game, offering personalized and data-driven recovery programs.

From advanced exoskeletons that aid in walking to VR-based cognitive therapy, these innovations are designed to restore independence and improve quality of life.

Restoring Mobility

One of the most promising developments is the use of bionic limbs that integrate directly with the user's nervous system, allowing for intuitive control and sensory feedback. This technology not only improves physical function but also helps in psychological recovery by restoring a sense of wholeness.

Centers across the country are now adopting these technologies, supported by new funding initiatives aimed at veteran healthcare.
